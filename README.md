**Program Type:** English Activity Class (Game-Based)  
**Recommended Age:** Kids (adjust language level as needed)

**Outcome:** Students practice English through movement, thinking, strategy, and teamwork.

## 1) Program Overview
In this class, students become **explorers** completing **4 missions**.  
Each student receives a **Golden Passport**. After finishing each mission, they earn a **stamp/sticker**.  
Students who collect all **4 stamps** receive a **prize** at the end.

---

## 2) Learning Objectives
By the end of the class, students will be able to:
- **Speak simple English commands** during active play
- **Listen and respond** quickly to teacher/team instructions
- Use **direction language** (left/right/forward/stop, etc.)
- Practice **team communication** and cooperative problem-solving
- Build confidence by speaking English in a fun, low-pressure setting

Success standard: **Participation and speaking effort**, not perfect grammar.

## 3) Materials & Setup Checklist
### Required
- Golden Passports (paper booklet or card)
- 4 stampers/stickers (one per station)
- Timer / stopwatch
- Whistle or “freeze” signal
- Station signs (Mission 1–4)

### By Station
**Air Bounce**
- Inflatable bounce / obstacle (or safe alternative)
- Queue markers (cones/tape)

**Power Brain Games**
- Flashcards / word cards
- Memory cards / matching set
- Mini-whiteboard + markers (optional)

**Laser Trap**
- Rope/string/tape to create “laser grid”
- Start/finish line tape
- Optional blindfold

**Laser Tag**
- Laser tag equipment or safe team-tag substitute
- Barriers/obstacles (boxes, soft blocks)
- Team bands (Red/Blue)

## 4) Class Formats & Timetable Options
### Option A (Recommended Full Program): 90–100 Minutes Rotation
- Introduction + Passport: 5–10 min  
- 4 Stations Rotation: 70–80 min (about 15–20 min each)  
- Wrap-up + Prize Ceremony: 5–10 min  

### Option B (Short Program): 60 Minutes (2 Stations)
- Warm-up: 10 min  
- Laser Tag: 20 min  
- Laser Trap: 20 min  
- Wrap-up: 10 min  

### Option C (Medium Program): 40–50 Minutes (2 Stations)
- Warm-up: 5–10 min  
- Air Bounce: 15–20 min  
- Power Brain: 15–20 min  
- Wrap-up: 5 min

## 5) The Passport System (How It Works)
### Teacher Script (Simple English)
“Today, you are explorers. You must complete **4 missions**.  
After each mission, you will get a **stamp**.  
If you collect all **4 stamps**, you will get a **prize**!”

### Rotation Method
- Divide students into small groups (4–6 per group recommended)
- Assign each group to a starting station
- Use a timer to rotate (e.g., every 15–20 minutes)
- After each station: stamp passports immediately

## 6) Mission Stations (4 Games)

# Mission 1: Air Bounce (Physical Mission)
### Objective
Students practice **action words** and **basic commands** while moving.

### English Focus
Action verbs + classroom commands

### Target Vocabulary
jump, run, climb, slide, fast, slow

### Sentence Targets
- “Jump!”
- “Run!”
- “Go fast!”
- “Climb up!”
- “Slide down!”

### Rule
Before entering, each student must say **at least 1 English word/sentence**.

### Reward
Stamp after completion.

---

# Mission 2: Power Brain Games (Thinking Mission)
### Objective
Students use English while solving simple tasks quickly.

### Format Ideas (choose 2–3 mini rounds)
- Word matching
- Memory flip game
- Simple quiz (“What is this?”)
- Speaking challenge (“Say 3 animals!”)

### English Focus
Vocabulary recall + short answers

### Reward
Stamp after completion.

---

# Mission 3: Laser Trap (Strategy Mission)
### Objective
Students pass through a “laser grid” while teammates guide using English.

### Setup
Create a grid with rope/string/tape. Students cross without touching.

### English Focus
Directions + listening

### Target Vocabulary
step, jump, crawl, slowly, careful, stop, forward, back, under, over

### Sentence Targets
- “Go forward.”
- “Stop!”
- “Go under.”
- “Jump over.”
- “Be careful!”
- “Slowly!”

### Rules
- Only English for guiding
- If someone touches a “laser,” restart from the start line (or lose 1 point)

### Variation (Optional)
Blindfold one student. Team must guide using **English only**.

### Reward
Stamp after completion.

---

# Mission 4: Laser Tag (Team Battle Mission)
### Objective
Students communicate in English during a team battle game.

### Setup
- 2 teams (Red vs Blue)
- Obstacles/barriers
- Give each team a mission (defend base / capture item / score points)

### English Focus
Commands + teamwork communication

### Target Vocabulary
attack, defend, go, stop, left, right, enemy, team, base, follow

### Sentence Targets
- “Go left!”
- “Go right!”
- “Follow me!”
- “Help me!”
- “I see an enemy!”
- “Protect the base!”

### Rules
- Only English allowed during the game  
- If students speak Korean: lose 1 life / 1 point (teacher chooses system)
- Bonus points for strong English teamwork

### Reward
Stamp after completion.

---

## 7) Teaching Method (Best Practice)
- Demonstrate first (show, then play)
- Use very simple English + gestures
- Repeat key phrases often
- Praise effort: “Good English!” “Nice try!”
- Encourage shy students with easy roles (“Say ‘Stop!’”)

---

## 8) Classroom Management & Safety
- Set clear rules before starting each mission
- Use a stop signal: “Freeze!” + whistle/clap
- Keep teams balanced
- Control movement and spacing (especially bounce + laser tag)
- Assign helpers/assistants if available

---

## 9) Wrap-up & Prize Ceremony (5–10 min)
### Steps
1. Check passports and count stamps  
2. Celebrate:
   “You completed all missions. Great job!”
3. Give rewards:
   - All 4 stamps = prize
   - Optional awards:
     - Best Speaker
     - Best Team Player
     - Most Active Student
